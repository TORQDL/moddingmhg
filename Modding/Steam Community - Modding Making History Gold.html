Why Mods?
Modded scenarios give Making History: Gold a longer life-span and allow you to do things that the base game will not.

Some examples are modern-day and WWI modded scenarios.












Here is an example of what mods can do using a WWI Mod.
Finding Mods
Mods can be found on the making history website under Scenarios > MH Gold
(http://making-history.com/scenario/all/1/mhgold/date/False)
Click on interesting sounding / looking scenarios and read the descriprions / comments to search for any unfixed bugs that could ruin the scenario for you.





This is the Deadlock mod, a very advanced scenario that adds the first world war to the game.





The top downloads section seems to have been broken with use of auto-clicks on certern scenarios so most of the best mods are not featured (such as Deadlock)
Adding Mods
Once mods have been downloaded, they will probably need to be unzipped and if the mod is an installer (A), it is reccomended that you set the path to an empty folder and manually add the mod yourself.
You should find the folder named after the mod (B)(in most cases it will just be alone in the zip file) and add it to:
Steamapps>common>Making History Gold>Modern Europe>system>scenarios




















(A) This is an example of an installer icon.















(B) This is what the inside of the folder will look like.
Playing Mods
Once in-game, the modded scenario will appear alongside the base game's scenarios.
If you have many mods (I have about 30) it is adviseable to keep them in a new folder and add them back into the game when you wish to play them to avoid having too big a list when searching for the scenario you wish to play in-game.
After this, you just play it like any other scenario.
